🎬 SFF Semantic Analysis Laboratory

Computational Modeling for Movie Festival Recommendation and Scheduling

1. Project Overview

This laboratory demonstrates a computational approach to resolving the "Information Overload" and "Cold Start" problems encountered during the 27th Shanghai International Film Festival. As a Mathematics and Applied Mathematics student, I developed this system to bridge the gap between abstract cinematic content and practical decision-making using high-dimensional vector spaces.

2. Interactive Lab (Live Demo)

Enter a movie title (e.g., The Grandmaster or Inception) below to see the semantic analysis in real-time. The system will retrieve the most semantically relevant films from the dataset.

3. Mathematical Methodology

The core of this system involves mapping unstructured film synopses into a 384-dimensional vector space. I utilized Sentence-BERT (S-BERT), specifically the all-MiniLM-L6-v2 transformer, to perform this feature extraction.

Once the text is vectorized, the similarity between films is quantified using Cosine Similarity. This measures the angular proximity between two vectors in the 384-dimensional manifold, rather than mere keyword overlap:

$$\text{similarity} = \cos(\theta) = \frac{\mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{B}}{\|\mathbf{A}\| \|\mathbf{B}\|}$$

This methodology ensures that recommendations are based on deep semantic context, allowing for a more nuanced understanding of film themes and genres.
